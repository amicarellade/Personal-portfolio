Golf Strokes Gained Calculator

Strokes Gained is a statistical measure used in golf to quantify a player's performance in various aspects of the game relative to the field average. It shows how many strokes a player gains or loses in categories like off-the-tee, approach-the-green, around-the-green, and putting.

Instructions:
1. Enter the par for each hole you played.
2. Enter the number of shots you took for each hole.
3. For each shot, enter the distance to the hole and the surface type (e.g., tee, fairway, rough, green).
4. Add or remove hole's depending on how many you played.
5. Click "Calculate Strokes Gained" to see your performance metrics.
6. Review the results to see your strokes gained in total and by category (off-the-tee, approach-the-green, around-the-green, putting, and penalties).

(Notice!) If you incurred any penalties (e.g., hitting out of bounds or into a hazard), this will not be calculated in your final SG calculations.

Category Descriptions

Strokes Gained: Off-the-Tee - Measures player performance off the tee on all par 4s and par 5s. This statistic looks at how much better or worse a playerâ€™s drive is compared to the average PGA Tour player.

Strokes Gained: Approach-the-Green - Measures player performance on approach shots and other shots that are not included in strokes gained: around-the-green and strokes gained: putting. It includes tee shots on par 3s.

Strokes Gained: Around-the-Green - Measures player performance on any shot within 30 yards of the edge of the green without measuring putting.

Strokes Gained: Putting - Measures how many strokes a player gains (or loses) on the greens compared to the PGA Tour average.

Strokes Gained: Tee-to-Green - The sum of strokes gained: off-the-tee, strokes gained: approach-the-green, and strokes gained: around-the-green.
